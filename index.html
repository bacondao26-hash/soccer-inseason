The Philosophy
Why in-season S&C matters
Most athletes stop lifting in-season because they're tired, busy, or their coach says training hard will make them slower. That's wrong on all three counts.
Research is clear: athletes who do zero S&C in-season lose 10–20% of their off-season strength gains in 6–8 weeks. Strength loss means slower acceleration, lower jump height, and a significantly higher injury risk — especially hamstring strains and ACL injuries — in the second half of the season.
Two sessions per week is all you need. The goal in-season is not to get fitter. It's to maintain what you built and stay healthy. Less volume than off-season, same intensity. That's how you finish the season faster and stronger than you started it.
Weekly Structure
How to schedule your week
With 2 games per week, S&C windows are tight. Here's the golden rule: never do strength work within 24 hours of a game, and never do speed work within 48 hours of a game.

Session A
Strength + Injury Prevention
~50 minutes · 2–3 days before your next game
Compound Strength
Hamstring Protection
Adductor Work
Core
Goal: Maintain the strength you built in pre-season. Keep the hamstrings and adductors loaded so they stay resilient through the full season. Short, sharp, and effective — no junk volume.
Warm-Up — 8 min
5 min light jog or bike → Leg swings (front/back + side/side, 10 each) → Bodyweight squat × 10 → Hip hinge × 10 → Lateral band walk × 10 each direction → Done. Go.
Block A — Lower Strength
A1
Trap Bar Deadlift
Your primary strength movement. Feet hip-width inside the bar. Push the floor away — don't think about pulling the bar up. Stand tall at the top. Hinge back down with control. This is the best single exercise for in-season lower body strength maintenance.
3 × 5
RPE 7–8
Rest 2 min
A2
Rear-Foot Elevated Split Squat (RFESS)
Back foot elevated on bench, DBs in hands. Drop straight down — don't lean forward, don't let your front knee cave in. Two sets only. This is single-leg loading in a fatigued in-season body — volume is intentionally low. Quality over quantity.
2 × 8 each
RPE 6–7
Rest 90s
Block B — Upper Maintenance
B1
DB Bench Press
Maintain strength — use the same weight as pre-season Phase 2 or close to it. Controlled descent (2s), explosive press. This is not the priority in-season, but neglecting it leads to upper body strength loss over a long season.
3 × 6–8
RPE 6–7
Rest 75s
B2
1-Arm DB Row
Knee and hand on bench. Pull elbow to hip. Keep torso still. This superpairs with bench — alternating keeps rest time efficient without rushing the session.
3 × 8 each
RPE 6
Rest 75s
Block C — Injury Prevention Non-Negotiable
C1
Nordic Hamstring Curl (Eccentric)
This one exercise reduces hamstring strain risk by up to 51% (van Dyk et al., meta-analysis). Kneel, partner holds ankles or hook under a fixed bar. Lower as slowly as possible — aim for 4 seconds. Catch with hands at the bottom. Assist back to top. The lowering phase is the entire point.
3 × 5
4s eccentric
Rest 90s
C2
Copenhagen Plank
Side plank, top foot or knee on bench. Hold. Adductor loading — the most evidence-backed groin injury prevention exercise in soccer. Do both sides every session, every week. This is your groin insurance policy for a long season.
3 × 20s each
Rest 45s
Block D — Core
D1
Dead Bug
On back, lower back pressed flat. Opposite arm + leg lower slowly, breathe out throughout. Core braced the entire time. This is spinal stability — the foundation all athletic movement comes from.
3 × 8 each
Rest 45s
D2
Pallof Press
Cable or band anchored to side. Press straight out from chest — hold 2 seconds — return. Body resists rotation throughout. Anti-rotation core training is directly specific to cutting, shielding, and change of direction — all major in-season demands.
3 × 10 each
Rest 45s
Session B
Speed + Power
~40 minutes · 2 days before your next game
Sprint Work
Box Jumps
Broad Jumps
Med Ball
Goal: Maintain your sprint speed and explosive power. Volume is low — quality is everything. Every rep must be 95%+ effort. If quality drops, the session ends. This is the session most in-season athletes skip. That's why they're slower in November than they were in September.
Sprint Warm-Up — 10 min (Required)
2 min easy jog → High knees 2×20yd → Butt kicks 2×20yd → A-march 2×20yd → Leg swings front/back + side/side → 4 build-ups: 40yd starting at 60%, building to 90% by the end. Walk back after each. This is not optional — cold muscles and sprinting is how hamstrings get pulled.
Sprint Block — Absolute max effort. Full rest between every single rep.
Speed Rule: Full Recovery Only
If you're not fully recovered, wait longer. Tired sprints train bad mechanics and accomplish nothing.
S1
10-Yard Sprint
Explosive standing start. Lean aggressively into your first step. Max effort on every rep. Walk back, take your time, go again when fully ready.
4–6 reps
Walk-back recovery
S2
20-Yard Sprint
Acceleration + early max velocity. Drive the first 10 yards, let your stride open up for the back 10. Relax your hands, jaw, and face — tension kills speed.
4 reps
Walk-back + 30s extra
S3
40-Yard Sprint
Max-velocity exposure. Two reps — full 3-minute rest between each. These are the most important reps of the week for maintaining your top-end speed. Don't skip them, don't rush them.
2 reps
3 min rest each
Power Block — Full recovery between sets
P1
Box Jump — Max Height
Max effort every rep. Full arm swing, full leg extension at takeoff. Land on box in a partial squat. Step down between reps (don't jump down). 90-second rest between sets — these are neuromuscular, not cardio.
3 × 4
90s between sets
P2
Broad Jump
Max distance every rep. Land in a stable athletic position — absorb the landing, don't crash into it. Soft landing = better deceleration mechanics = fewer knee injuries in-season.
3 × 3
90s between sets
P3
Med Ball Rotational Throw (Wall)
Stand side-on to wall, 3–4 ft away. Load the back hip, explode from the hips (not the arms), and throw hard into the wall. Hip power — directly specific to shooting, turning, and winning physical duels. 4 reps each side.
3 × 4 each side
60s between sets
Cool-Down — 5 min
90/90 hip stretch × 60s each side → Standing quad stretch × 30s each → Standing hip flexor stretch × 30s each → Walk 2 min. Done.
Week by Week
4-week progression
This 4-week block can be repeated for as long as your season runs. Deload at Week 4, then restart Week 1 slightly heavier.
WeekSession A — StrengthSession B — Speed
Week 1 Establish baseline loads. RPE 7 on Trap Bar DL. Keep prehab volume exactly as written. 6×10yd, 4×20yd, 2×40yd. Quality over quantity. If tired, cut 2 reps from 10yd set.
Week 2 Add 5–10 lbs to Trap Bar DL and RFESS where RPE allows. Prehab stays the same. Same volume as Week 1. Focus on quality and explosiveness — time yourself on the 40yd if possible.
Week 3 Maintain Week 2 loads. If match load is high, reduce RFESS to 1×8 each. Nordic and Copenhagen stay. Same volume. If legs are heavy from matches, cut 40yd reps to 1. Never skip the 10yd work.
Week 4 — Deload Reduce sets by 1 on all lifts. Keep intensity (same weight). Nordic and Copenhagen stay at full dose. 4×10yd, 3×20yd only. No 40yd sprints. Light, sharp, done in 25 min. Legs freshen up for next block.
Repeating the block: After Week 4 deload, go back to Week 1 and add 5–10 lbs to your Trap Bar DL baseline. Your sprint times should be similar or faster. If they're getting slower over the season, sleep and recovery are the first things to address.
Non-Negotiables
In-season rules
  • 🏃Session B before Session A in the training week. Speed work needs a fresher nervous system than strength work. If you can only do one session this week, do Session B.
  • ⚽Games take priority. If you're exhausted 48 hours before a game, skip or shorten the session. Arriving fresh to the game is always the right call.
  • 🔴Never skip the prehab block. Nordic curls and Copenhagen planks in Block C are the most important exercises in this program. A hamstring strain or groin pull ends your season — 10 minutes of prevention every session is non-negotiable.
  • ⚡Sprint quality over sprint quantity. Five perfect 10-yard sprints at 100% are worth more than ten tired ones at 70%. If you're not fully recovered between reps, wait longer.
  • 😴Sleep is your number one recovery tool. With 2 games per week and 2 S&C sessions, you're accumulating high load. 8–9 hours is not a luxury — it's part of the program.
  • 🍽️Eat for your load. Match weeks are high-calorie weeks. Skimping on carbs before games and protein after sessions is the fastest way to feel flat. Fuel the work you're doing.
Health First
When to stop and get help
Muscle soreness is normal. These are not:
  • 🚨Sharp pain in the back of the thigh during sprints or Nordic curls — stop immediately. Could be a hamstring strain.
  • 🚨Groin pain during Copenhagen planks or kicking — reduce load and get it assessed.
  • 🚨Knee pain that worsens during or after squatting — don't push through it. Assess before the next session.
  • 🚨Any pain that gets worse rep-by-rep during a set — rack the weight and stop that exercise for the day.
When in doubt — DM @performance.bacon. I'll tell you whether to push through, modify, or refer out. Don't guess.
